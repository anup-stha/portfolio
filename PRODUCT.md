# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, design leads, and founders evaluating a design engineer for roles or engagements. They arrive from a link (CV, LinkedIn, referral), skim in under two minutes on desktop or phone, and decide whether to dig into case studies or reach out. Secondary: peers and collaborators checking craft.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for a design engineer: someone who designs products and ships the front-end code. The site itself is the primary proof of skill — its typography, motion, and interaction quality must demonstrate the craft it claims. Success = visitor scrolls the full page, feels the care, and opens a case study or the contact link.

## Brand Personality

Playful, precise, warm. Toy-like delight (physics pill pile, googly eyes, marquees) sitting on rigorous typographic and motion craft. The tension between "fun" and "exact" IS the brand: every playful element must be executed with engineering precision. Voice: first-person, plain, specific; quietly confident, never salesy.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS/template portfolios: hero-metric blocks, identical card grids, uppercase tracked eyebrows over every section.
- Dev-portfolio clichés: terminal themes, matrix green, "passionate about clean code" copy.
- Sterile minimalism that deletes the personality (the pills, eyes, and serif moments stay).
- AI-slop tells: gradient text, glassmorphism, aphoristic copy cadence.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the case study.** Every interaction (pill physics, card stacking, cursor-tracking eyes) doubles as evidence of design-engineering skill, so each must be flawless at 60fps.
2. **Playful surface, exact bones.** Pastels and toys ride on a strict type scale, spacing rhythm, and contrast discipline.
3. **Motion tells the story.** Scroll and load choreography guide reading order; nothing animates without a purpose, and everything respects reduced-motion.
4. **Serif = identity moments.** The display serif appears only at identity peaks (hero statement, More/Work interlude, footer wordmark); sans carries information.
5. **Placeholder today, real tomorrow.** Structure and copy patterns must survive the swap to real projects without redesign.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, visible focus states on all interactive elements, full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives (crossfades instead of drops/marquees), semantic landmarks, alt/aria on decorative SVGs (`aria-hidden`) and meaningful links.
